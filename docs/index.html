Sumatra-Andaman

Indonesia - 26 December 2004, at 07:58 local time

The Sumatra-Andaman earthquake occurred on December 26, 2004, off the coast of northern Sumatra, Indonesia. It had a magnitude of 9.1-9.3 and triggered devastating tsunamis across the Indian Ocean. The earthquake and ensuing tsunamis caused widespread destruction and claimed over 230,000 lives in 14 countries. It led to advancements in tsunami warning systems and increased global awareness of the need for preparedness and response to such disasters.

Great East Japan Earthquake

Japan - 11 March 2011, at 14:46 local time

The Great East Japan Earthquake struck Japan on March 11, 2011. With a magnitude of 9.0-9.1, it triggered massive tsunamis that caused widespread devastation. Approximately 15,000 lives were lost, and the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster occurred. The event emphasized the importance of earthquake preparedness and response measures.
